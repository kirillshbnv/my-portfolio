Who Am I?

My name is Kirill Shabanov. I am a third-year Computer Science student at Mount Royal University in Calgary, Alberta.


I'm drawn to systems, the ones we build, the ones we live in, and the ones we're only beginning to understand. To me, code isn't just a tool. It's a way of shaping possibility and giving structure to new ideas. I'm not just here to write software. I want to help build what comes next.

I'm fascinated by the space where theory meets reality, where abstract logic becomes usable technology. Whether I'm crafting internal tools, refining backend systems, or shaping user-facing features, I bring clarity, precision, and purpose to everything I build.

I build to give ideas weight. I learn to bring the future within reach. I code because inside every line of logic is a chance to do something that matters.


Below, you'll find a glimpse of the kind of developer I am and the kind of person I'm becoming.

What I'm Working On

I'm currently developing a 2D arcade-style game for the Atari ST, called Snake Frenzy, written in C and optimized with Motorola 68K Assembly. It's a deep dive into low-level systems and performance optimization, pushing me to write efficient, hardware-aware code.

In parallel, I'm building client-facing websites using semantic HTML, with plans for responsive CSS and JavaScript integration. These projects challenge me to deliver clean, maintainable code that solves real problems for real users.

I'm also deepening my backend knowledge, exploring APIs, databases, and scalable architectures. Revisiting fundamentals like data structures and design principles continues to sharpen my thinking and expand my capabilities.

My Development Philosophy

I don't just want to write code. I want to make the improbable real. To me, programming isn't just syntax. It's a way to carve clarity from complexity. It's how theory becomes impact.

I'm drawn to the edge, where performance matters, where precision is unforgiving, where each decision ripples across the system. That's where I feel most alive.
